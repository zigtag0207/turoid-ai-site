Short answer

How to compare software

Evaluate whether a tool can close a period reliably—not just display charts—then assess expansion into documents and AI workflows.

Step 1

Clarify your operating model

Before comparing vendors, define your workflows and outputs.

Questions to answer

  • Who owns reporting and data quality?
  • What monthly/quarterly outputs are required?
  • What must be reconciled before publishing?
  • Which workflows are document-driven (alts, statements, notices)?
  • What controls/approvals must exist for auditability?

Step 2

Compare by capability (not by brand)

Use capability pages to evaluate workflow depth.

Document AI

 Capability

Controls

Step 3

Implementation reality check

Most projects fail due to unclear ownership, scope, and period-close definitions.

Evaluation scorecard

  • Data ingest + normalization across sources
  • Reconciliation + exception handling workflows
  • Reporting packs + multi-entity/principal views
  • Alternatives support + document workflows
  • Controls + approvals + audit trail
  • Security posture + permissions model
  • Time to first “closed period”
