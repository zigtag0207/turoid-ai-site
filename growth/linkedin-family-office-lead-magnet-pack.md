## Post 2: AI Governance

The first useful AI project for a family office is often not "ask a chatbot about the portfolio."

It is more basic and more valuable:

Can AI read the documents the team already handles every month?

Examples:

- private-bank statements
- capital call notices
- distribution notices
- fund reports
- manager letters
- investment committee materials

But for family offices, the key is not extraction alone.

The key is source evidence, review queues, exceptions, approvals, and auditability.

That is where AI moves from interesting to operationally useful.

## Post 3: Excel Risk

Excel is not the enemy in a family office.

Uncontrolled Excel is.

The question is not "should we stop using spreadsheets?"

The question is:

Which spreadsheets are analysis files, and which spreadsheets have become operating controls?

If a workbook decides what goes into the monthly report, tracks private fund commitments, explains exceptions, or acts as the source of truth after approvals, it is no longer just a spreadsheet.

It is part of the operating system.

That is usually the right place to start a software conversation.

## Post 4: Alternatives

Alternative investments create a very specific family office software problem.

The data does not arrive cleanly through one pipe.

It arrives as:

- capital call notices

- NAV statements
- manager letters
- fund reports
- emails
- signed documents
- spreadsheets

If the office cannot connect those documents to commitments, exposures, reporting, and approvals, alternatives remain a manual workflow even when the rest of the portfolio looks digital.
