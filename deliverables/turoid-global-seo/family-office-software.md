# Family Office Software for Modern Wealth Teams

Turoid is family office software for complex portfolios and multi-entity structures—built to consolidate data, produce reliable reporting, reduce reconciliation drift, and automate document-heavy operations with private, workflow-aware AI.

## What family office software should do (and why spreadsheets break)

- Consolidate positions, transactions, and cash across custodians and private banks.
- Keep an auditable truth set for reporting (before “nice charts”).
- Handle alternative investments without manual roll-forwards and copy/paste.
- Reduce operational risk with controls, approvals, and exception workflows.
- Turn documents into structured data and routed tasks.

## What Turoid is

Turoid combines an operating layer for portfolio operations (data + workflows) with an AI layer that is designed to be grounded in your internal context (documents, manager notes, calendars, and approved data).

## FAQ

### What is family office software?
Software that consolidates portfolio data, supports reporting and operational workflows, and adds controls so teams can run repeatable processes without spreadsheet drift.

### How is it different from portfolio management software?
Portfolio management tools often focus on analytics and dashboards; family office software must also handle multi-entity operational workflows, reconciliation, controls, documents, and reporting packs.

### How do you handle alternatives?
By supporting structured cashflows, valuations, documents, and exception workflows so reporting doesn’t depend on manual roll-forwards.

### How do you manage reconciliation across custodians?
By normalizing records, matching positions/transactions/cash, routing breaks to owners, and preserving an audit trail from ingest to resolution.

### How long does implementation take?
Depends on data sources and desired outputs; start with a narrow scope and expand once the reconciliation/reporting model is stable.

### Can we support multiple entities and ownership structures?
Yes—family offices typically need entity-level, principal-level, and consolidated views.
