# Family Office Software Comparison Guide (2026)

This guide helps wealth teams compare family office software based on how family offices actually operate: reconciliation first, reporting outputs, alternatives, documents, and controls—not just dashboards.

**CTA:** Request a demo  
Start here: `/family-office-software/`

## Step 1: Clarify your operating model

- Who owns reporting and data quality?
- What outputs are required each month/quarter?
- What must be reconciled before outputs are published?
- What workflows are document-driven (alts, statements, notices)?

## Step 2: Compare by capability (not by brand)

### Reporting depth
What outputs can be produced, and are they auditable?

Link: `/family-office-reporting-software/`

### Reconciliation workflow
Can you prevent drift across custodians and managers?

Link: `/family-office-reconciliation-software/`

### Alternative investments support
How do documents and timing differences flow into reporting?

Link: `/family-office-document-ai/`

### Controls and approvals
Can you prevent “spreadsheet overrides” from becoming permanent risk?

Link: `/family-office-controls-approvals/`

## Step 3: Implementation reality check

- What data sources must be connected (banks, custodians, administrators)?
- What’s the minimum viable scope for the first 6–8 weeks?
- Who is the internal product owner?

## A simple evaluation scorecard (copy/paste)

- Data ingest + normalization
- Reconciliation + exception handling
- Reporting packs + multi-entity views
- Alternatives support + document workflows
- Controls + approvals + audit trail
- Security posture + permissions model
- Time to first “closed period”

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Buying dashboards before reconciliation is stable
- Ignoring document-heavy workflows (alts)
- No internal owner for the operating model
